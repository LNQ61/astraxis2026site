Pharma Logistics Is Becoming a Strategic Gatekeeper for Asia Suppliers

For Singapore market entry, product fit is only part of the requirement.

In February 2026, DHL announced an expanded dedicated Airfreight Cold Chain Network for healthcare products, including temperature-sensitive medicines, vaccines, pharmaceutical products, and cell and gene therapies.

For suppliers entering Singapore pharma, this is an important signal. Buyers assess product fit together with the supplier's ability to support reliable movement, temperature control, visibility, documentation, and escalation.

For Singapore pharma and lab teams, logistics reliability is becoming part of sourcing quality. A technically suitable product may still fail commercially if the supplier cannot support compliant, predictable movement into Singapore.

Why it matters to Astraxis

Astraxis supports this requirement by helping suppliers understand Singapore expectations and helping buyers source from Asia with clearer control over response time, documentation, and service follow-up.
