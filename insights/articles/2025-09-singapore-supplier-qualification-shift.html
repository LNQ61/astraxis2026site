Singapore Pharma Sites Are Raising the Bar on Supplier Qualification

What procurement and quality teams in Tuas are now expecting from regional suppliers — and why the old approach is no longer enough.

Astraxis International

Singapore's pharma manufacturing base accelerated sharply in 2025. AstraZeneca broke ground on its USD 1.5 billion end-to-end ADC manufacturing facility in Tuas South in November 2024, and the pipeline of investment commitments from major manufacturers continued to grow through the year. With this scale of build-out comes heightened operational scrutiny — including from procurement and quality teams who are selecting and qualifying regional suppliers for the environments they are building.

The ISPE Singapore Affiliate defines GMP compliance and supplier qualification as foundational requirements for any supplier engaged in pharma manufacturing environments. As Tuas sites ramp up production and headcount, these expectations are being applied more consistently to regional sourcing — not only to direct materials, but to equipment, consumables, and service providers operating within or adjacent to GMP environments. The standard is no longer reserved for large global vendors.

What this means in practice: suppliers are being asked for ISO certificates, Certificates of Analysis, delivery records, and technical datasheets earlier in the conversation. Paper-based or informal supplier relationships that worked in earlier phases are being replaced by structured qualification processes. Documentation gaps that were once tolerated at the sample or trial stage are now causing suppliers to be passed over. For Asia-based suppliers without Singapore-facing documentation, this creates a clear barrier to entry — regardless of product quality.

The shift is not only about compliance. Singapore procurement teams are operating under tighter timelines and with less tolerance for supplier follow-up that requires repeated chasing. A supplier who can respond promptly, provide complete documentation on first request, and demonstrate familiarity with Singapore site expectations has a material commercial advantage over one who cannot — even if the underlying product is comparable.
